SUICA Card Guide

Your essential transportation card in Tokyo

What is SUICA?

SUICA is a rechargeable IC card that makes traveling around Tokyo incredibly convenient. Simply tap your card on the reader at station gates and it automatically deducts the fare. No need to buy individual tickets or worry about calculating fares!

All Trains & Buses

Works on JR, subway, and most buses throughout Tokyo and beyond

Shops & Vending Machines

Pay at convenience stores, vending machines, and many restaurants

Fast & Convenient

No more fumbling with tickets. Just tap and go!

How to Get Your SUICA Card

Welcome SUICA

For: Foreign visitors
Where: Haneda & Narita airports, major JR stations
Cost: ¥2,000 (¥1,500 usable + ¥500 deposit)
Validity: 28 days from purchase

Non-personalized card perfect for short stays. No refund hassle. Look for "Welcome SUICA" signs at airports.

Regular SUICA

For: Anyone
Where: JR ticket machines and counters
Cost: ¥1,000-10,000 (includes ¥500 deposit)
Validity: 10 years

Standard card with deposit refund. You get the deposit back minus ¥220 handling fee when you return it.

Step-by-Step: Buying SUICA at the Airport

At Haneda or Narita Airport

  1. Find a JR Ticket Machine: Look for machines with "IC Card" or "SUICA" labels. Blue machines are JR East.
  2. Select Language: Tap the language button in the corner to change to English.
  3. Choose "Purchase New SUICA": Select "Welcome SUICA" option (recommended for tourists).
  4. Select Amount: Welcome SUICA: ¥2,000. Regular SUICA: Choose from ¥1,000, ¥2,000, ¥5,000, or ¥10,000.
  5. Insert Money: Pay with cash (¥1,000, ¥5,000, ¥10,000 notes accepted) or credit card.
  6. Receive Your Card: Take your card and receipt. Your card is ready to use immediately!
Digital Option

iPhone and Apple Watch users can add SUICA to Apple Pay. Android users can use Mobile SUICA app. However, purchasing a physical card at the airport is easier for first-time visitors.

How to Use Your SUICA Card

Using on Trains

  1. At Entry: Hold card over the blue reader at the ticket gate. Listen for the beep and check the green light.
  2. Current Balance Displayed: After tapping, your remaining balance appears on the display.
  3. At Exit: Tap the reader again when exiting. Fare is automatically deducted.
  4. If Balance Low: Use the fare adjustment machine near exits to add more credit.

Using in Shops

  1. At Checkout: Say "SUICA de" (SU-i-ca-day) meaning "by SUICA".
  2. Hold Over Reader: Place card on the payment terminal (often has a penguin icon).
  3. Wait for Confirmation: Listen for the beep and check for the green light or "OK" message.
  4. Take Receipt: The amount is deducted automatically. Check your balance on the receipt.

Recharging Your SUICA

Where to Recharge

  • Ticket Machines: At any JR or subway station. Most accept cash (up to ¥10,000 notes).
  • Convenience Stores: 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson. Ask staff "Charge kudasai" (char-ju ku-da-sai).
  • Station Counters: Staffed ticket counters can help if you have questions.

How to Recharge at Machines

  1. Insert your SUICA card into the slot
  2. Select "Charge" or "チャージ" (Charge in Japanese)
  3. Choose amount: ¥500, ¥1,000, ¥2,000, ¥3,000, ¥5,000, or ¥10,000
  4. Insert cash
  5. Take your card and receipt
How Much to Charge?

Start with ¥2,000-3,000. Average daily transportation costs in Tokyo: ¥500-1,500. You can always add more as needed. Maximum balance is ¥20,000.

Important Tips

Do's

  • Keep card easily accessible
  • Check balance regularly
  • Recharge before balance runs low
  • Use at convenience stores for snacks
  • Keep card safe—it's like cash!

Don'ts

  • Don't put near phones/magnets
  • Don't bend or damage the card
  • Don't share one card between multiple people at gates
  • Don't forget to tap out at your destination
  • Don't lose it—balance isn't recoverable!
Alternative: PASMO Card

PASMO is another IC card that works exactly like SUICA. They're interchangeable throughout Tokyo. If SUICA machines are busy, you can buy PASMO at Tokyo Metro stations. Same convenience, same functionality!
