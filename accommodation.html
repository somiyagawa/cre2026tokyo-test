Where to Stay

Accommodation Guide

From budget hostels to luxury hotels

Finding the Perfect Place to Stay

Tokyo offers accommodation options for every budget. We recommend staying near major train lines for easy access to the conference venue at Myogadani Station (Marunouchi Line).

Budget Options (¥3,000-6,000/night)

Capsule Hotels

Price: ¥3,000-5,000/night
Best For: Solo travelers, unique experience

Compact sleeping pods with shared facilities. Clean, safe, and uniquely Japanese. Most include WiFi, lounge areas, and bath facilities.

Recommended:
• Nine Hours (multiple locations)
• First Cabin (Akihabara, Asakusa)
• The Millennials (Shibuya)

Hostels

Price: ¥3,500-6,000/night
Best For: Budget travelers, meeting people

Dormitory-style accommodation with shared spaces. Great for socializing with international travelers. Most offer private rooms too.

Recommended:
• K's House Tokyo (Kuramae)
• Nui. Hostel & Bar (Kuramae)
• Sakura Hotel Ikebukuro

Mid-Range Options (¥8,000-15,000/night)

Business Hotels

Price: ¥8,000-12,000/night

Compact, efficient rooms with private bathrooms. Clean and comfortable. Often include breakfast.

Chains:
• Toyoko Inn
• APA Hotels
• Super Hotel
• Dormy Inn

City Hotels

Price: ¥10,000-15,000/night

Larger rooms with more amenities. Good locations near major stations. Professional service.

Examples:
• Hotel Gracery
• Sotetsu Hotels
• Mitsui Garden Hotels

Guesthouses

Price: ¥8,000-12,000/night

Japanese-style accommodations, often family-run. More personal experience with cultural insights.

Features:
• Tatami rooms
• Japanese breakfast
• Local insights

Comfort & Luxury (¥15,000+/night)

Upscale Hotels

Price: ¥15,000-25,000/night

Spacious rooms, excellent service, premium amenities. Often include restaurants, bars, and fitness facilities.

Options:
• Hotel Metropolitan
• Keio Plaza Hotel
• Century Southern Tower
• The B Hotels

Luxury Hotels

Price: ¥30,000+/night

Five-star accommodations with world-class service. Premium locations with spectacular views.

Choices:
• Park Hyatt Tokyo
• The Peninsula Tokyo
• Aman Tokyo
• Mandarin Oriental

Recommended Areas to Stay

Near the Venue

Ikebukuro, Korakuen, Ochanomizu
Close to Myogadani via Marunouchi Line. Convenient for daily commute to conference.

Central Tokyo

Tokyo Station, Nihombashi
Excellent train access to all areas. Easy connection to Marunouchi Line.

Entertainment Districts

Shinjuku, Shibuya, Roppongi
Vibrant nightlife and dining. 20-30 minutes to venue by train.

Traditional Areas

Asakusa, Ueno
Cultural atmosphere with temples and museums. Good train connections.

Booking Tips

Important Advice

  • Book Early: July is summer vacation season in Japan. Popular hotels fill up quickly.
  • Check Location: Ensure your hotel has easy access to the Marunouchi Line or major transfer stations.
  • Room Size: Japanese hotel rooms are typically smaller than Western standards. Check dimensions.
  • Cancellation Policy: Many Japanese hotels have strict cancellation policies. Read carefully.
  • English Support: Major chains and international hotels have English-speaking staff.
  • Booking Platforms: Use Booking.com, Agoda, Rakuten Travel, or Jalan.net for best rates.
Peak Season Alert

The conference dates (July 11-15) fall during summer travel season. We strongly recommend booking accommodation as soon as your attendance is confirmed. Consider staying slightly outside central Tokyo for better availability and rates.
